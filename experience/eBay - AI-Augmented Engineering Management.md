# eBay - AI-Augmented Engineering Management

## Situation

As eBay expanded AI adoption across the engineering org (ChatGPT, Claude Code, Glean, NotebookLM), engineering managers faced a choice: use AI to generate polished documents, or use it to reclaim meaningful time and invest it in higher-leverage people work. The admin layer of EM work — status updates, resume screening, performance reviews, quarterly planning, stakeholder communication — was consuming a disproportionate share of calendar time without creating compounding value.

## Task

Systematically embed AI into my own engineering management workflows across six areas: upward management, customer management, performance reviews, hiring pipeline, project management, and remote team onboarding — while preserving the human judgment that AI cannot replace.

## Action

- **Upward management**: Built a workflow to transform raw notes into executive summaries emphasizing outcomes over implementation; QBR preparation dropped from a full day to hours; weekly status updates from 1–2 hours to 15 minutes
- **Incident & customer management**: Deployed custom agents and skills to reduce team toil by 50%; incidents dropped from 3–4/month to ~1; triage agents handled 70% of customer support cases autonomously
- **Performance reviews**: Used NotebookLM and Glean to organize 1-on-1 notes and career framework history, enabling evidence-based reviews instead of memory-dependent assessments; engineers reported feeling genuinely seen
- **Hiring pipeline**: Built structured AI skills for screening, question generation, and feedback aggregation; interview overhead dropped from 3–4 hours per interview to ~90 minutes; hiring cycle compressed from 3+ months to 4–6 weeks
- **Project management**: Shifted quarterly planning from debate-heavy sessions to 30-minute alignment checks with AI-generated transparent documentation; OKR completion improved from ~50% to ~80%
- **Remote onboarding**: Built structured documentation reducing new-hire context requests; time-to-productivity dropped from 3 months to 6 weeks
- **Maintained judgment hygiene**: Deliberately formed opinions before consulting AI; used AI as thinking partner, not replacement; avoided over-reliance on tool breadth (depth in 2–3 core workflows)

## Result

- Admin layer of EM work reduced to roughly one-third of previous time
- Hiring: 8+ engineers hired across Europe and India within 3 months; pipeline of 200–300 candidates managed efficiently
- Incidents: 3–4/month → ~1/month (50%+ reduction); MTTD ~20 min, MTTR ~1–2 hours
- Customer support: 70% of cases handled autonomously by triage agents
- OKR completion: ~50% → ~80%
- New-hire ramp: 3 months → 6 weeks
- Hiring skills and ramp-up agents adopted by other teams across the org

## Key Questions

**Q: How do you decide which parts of your EM workflow to automate versus keep human?**
Talking points: Identify tasks that are high-frequency but low-judgment (status summaries, resume filtering, OKR drafts); preserve human judgment where trust and nuance matter most (1-on-1 conversations, performance calibration, final hiring decisions); treat AI as a thinking partner, not a replacement — form your own view first.

**Q: Tell me about a time you significantly improved your hiring process.**
Talking points: Name the bottleneck (3–4 hours per interview, 3+ month cycle); describe the specific changes (AI skills for screening, question gen, feedback aggregation); quantify the result (90-minute interviews, 4–6 week cycle, 8+ hires across two regions in 3 months).

**Q: How do you improve OKR completion rates on your team?**
Talking points: Diagnose why completion was low (~50%) — debate-heavy planning, unclear accountability; shift to AI-generated transparent documentation with 30-minute alignment checks; track completion quarter over quarter; 50% → 80% is the concrete evidence.

**Q: How do you run effective performance reviews at scale?**
Talking points: Avoid memory-dependent assessments — use structured 1-on-1 notes and career framework tracking throughout the year; AI helps organize evidence, not generate conclusions; engineers feeling genuinely seen is the qualitative signal that the process worked.

## Summary

This story documents a systematic effort to reclaim EM time from administrative overhead and reinvest it in higher-leverage people work. The insight driving it was that AI should compress the time cost of low-judgment tasks — not replace the judgment itself. Across six EM workflows (upward management, incident response, performance reviews, hiring, project planning, and onboarding), I built targeted AI-assisted processes that each produced measurable improvements: hiring cycle from 3+ months to 4–6 weeks, OKRs from 50% to 80% completion, incidents from 3–4/month to ~1/month, and new-hire ramp from 3 months to 6 weeks.

The meta-lesson — deliberately forming opinions before consulting AI and using it as a thinking partner rather than an answer generator — is what makes this story durable. It demonstrates both the practical results and the judgment about where human oversight remains essential, which is the nuanced answer interviewers at AI companies are actually looking for.
