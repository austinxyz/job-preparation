## Core Concepts
- **Situation**: Set the scene — context, scale, constraints. Be specific. （背景情境）
- **Task**: Your responsibility — what were YOU accountable for? （你的职责）
- **Action**: What YOU did. Use "I", not "we". Focus on decisions and trade-offs. （个人行动，强调"I"而非"we"）
- **Result**: Quantify. Revenue, time saved, reliability improved, team grew. （量化结果）
- Common mistakes: too much Situation, not enough Action; saying "we" throughout; no numbers in Result. （常见失误：情境过多、行动不足、结果不量化）

## Key Questions

### Conflict & Disagreement
- **Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager.** Framework: acknowledge their goal as valid → bring data or outside input to reopen the solution space → propose path satisfying both constraints.
- **How do you handle disagreements on technical decisions with teammates?** Framework: active listening → data-driven argument → openness to alternate perspectives → escalate via design doc or prototype if needed.
- **Describe a time when you pushed back on a decision you thought was wrong.** Framework: state the business risk clearly → propose alternative → be willing to commit once a decision is made.
- **Tell me about a time someone pushed you to your limit. How did you react?** Framework: emotional intelligence, self-control, reflection; what you'd do differently.

### Influence Without Authority
- **Describe a time you had to convince another team to adopt a more secure or technically complex design despite pushback.** Framework: understand their constraint → frame change as reducing their risk → use data and a pilot/proof-of-concept to de-risk.
- **When you have difficulty persuading someone, what do you do?** Framework: adaptability — try different angles (data, empathy, stakeholder alignment, partial agreement).
- **Tell me about a time when team priorities conflicted with another team's. How did you resolve it?** Framework: proactive communication, find shared goals, negotiate a phased agreement.

### Leadership & Initiative
- **Describe a time you saw others not acting and took it upon yourself to lead.** Framework: initiative, accountability, driving outcomes without being asked.
- **Share a time you had to provide clear direction to a group. How did you ensure alignment?** Framework: clarity of communication, checking for understanding, single-source-of-truth docs, follow-up.
- **Tell me about a large-scale technical change you drove. What did you learn?** Framework: vision → alignment → phased delivery → handling resistance → outcome.

### Adaptability & Learning
- **Tell me about a time when you had to learn a new technology quickly to complete a project.** Framework: fast learning, seeking resources, applying knowledge under time pressure.
- **Describe a time you had to adapt your plan when conditions changed.** Framework: recognize the signal early, re-prioritize ruthlessly, communicate the pivot to stakeholders.

### Team Dynamics & Collaboration
- **Describe a time when you had to work with a cross-functional team to achieve a goal.** Framework: role clarity, managing diverse stakeholders, aligning on shared objective, outcome.
- **Tell me about a time you mentored a colleague. How did you approach it?** Framework: assess knowledge gaps, tailor coaching style, measure growth, give credit.
- **How do you give and receive feedback in a high-performing team?** Framework: specific, timely, behavior-focused; openness to feedback; continuous improvement mindset.

### Customer Centricity & Stakeholder Management
- **Describe a time when you identified a customer pain point and implemented a solution.** Framework: empathy-first investigation → SLO/success-metric alignment → solution delivery → feedback loop.
- **Describe a time when you had to convey bad news to a team or stakeholder.** Framework: transparency, empathy, focus on path forward, maintain trust.
- **Tell me about a team situation with diverse stakeholders across orgs. How did you navigate it?** Framework: political awareness, stakeholder map, alignment strategy, effective communication channels.

### Values & Integrity
- **Describe an unpopular decision you made and its outcome.** Framework: courage, standing by decisions, stakeholder management, learning from the result.
- **Describe a time you stood up for a secure design even when it slowed development.** Framework: integrity, advocacy for quality, clear risk communication.
- **What would you do in your first 90 days in this role?** Framework: learn (customers, team, systems), build relationships (1:1s), identify quick wins, draft a 90-day plan with measurable goals.

## Summary
The STAR method structures behavioral interview answers to be specific, verifiable, and impactful. The Action section is the most important — interviewers want to understand your decision-making process, not just the outcome. Quantify results whenever possible (e.g., "reduced training time by 40%", "grew team from 5 to 12 engineers").

> 面试重点：Action 部分最关键，体现决策过程和 trade-off；Result 必须量化，用数字说话
